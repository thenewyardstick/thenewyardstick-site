Citroën Guide

TNY 17 Jul 2026

Citroën has reinvented itself as one of Europe's leading value-focused car brands, offering distinctive styling, generous equipment and family-friendly practicality at competitive prices. From the tiny Ami quadricycle to practical family SUVs and MPVs, the range prioritises comfort and affordability over outright performance. Many models are now available with petrol, hybrid or electric powertrains, while clever packaging and keen pricing help Citroën stand out in an increasingly crowded market.

The Ami is unlike anything else on sale, offering affordable electric mobility for short urban journeys. Classified as a quadricycle rather than a conventional car, it's compact, inexpensive to run and remarkably easy to park. It won't suit longer trips, but for city centres or private estates it makes plenty of sense.

RRP From : £21,475
Pre Reg From : c. £18,000
Used Price From : c. £16,500
Powertrains : Petrol, Hybrid, EV
Dimensions : 4395mm × 1795mm × 1660mm
Boot : 460L (40L 7-seat)

The C3 Aircross offers impressive interior space and, uniquely in its class, the option of seven seats. Generous standard equipment, efficient hybrid and electric powertrains and competitive pricing make it one of the strongest value-focused family SUVs, even if some rivals offer sharper handling.

Boot : C4: 380L / C4 X: 510L

The C4 combines hatchback practicality with crossover styling, while the C4 X adds a larger boot and fastback-inspired silhouette. Both prioritise comfort over outright Performance and are available with efficient hybrid and electric powertrains, making them sensible choices for buyers looking for relaxed everyday transport.
