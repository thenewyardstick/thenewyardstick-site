Could These Two Models Complete Suzuki's Future Line-up?

✎ Design Studio 23 Jul 2026

Suzuki's range has become one of the simplest on sale, but two clear opportunities remain. At the bottom sits the biggest omission of all: there's no modern successor to cars like the Alto, Celerio or Ignis.

An affordable city car has long been one of Suzuki's greatest strengths, and an electric replacement could not only restore that missing rung of the ladder, but also help the brand meet increasingly demanding European emissions regulations.

Meanwhile, the Vitara and S-Cross are beginning to show their age. Replacing both with a single new hybrid SUV would create a more logical bridge between the e Vitara and Across.


The Japanese-market e-Sky demonstrates how Suzuki could return to the kei car segment, but Europe presents a different challenge. Rather than starting again, Suzuki could adapt the same design into a slightly larger global model, preserving its compact footprint while improving refinement, luggage space and everyday usability.


A modest electric motor wouldn't be a compromise – it would be entirely in keeping with Suzuki's philosophy. The Alto, Celerio and Ignis were never bought for their performance, but for their dependability, low running costs and ability to make the most of every centimetre of road and parking space.


Higher up the range, Suzuki faces a different challenge. The Vitara and S-Cross have served the brand well, but after years on sale the two SUVs have become increasingly similar in size and purpose. With the all-electric e Vitara now covering buyers ready to make the switch to EVs, Suzuki has an opportunity to simplify its line-up.

Replacing both models with a single hybrid-only S-Cross would reduce complexity and create a clearer choice for customers.

Growing to around 4.4 metres long, the new S-Cross would become Suzuki's mainstay family SUV. Positioned below the larger plug-in hybrid Across, which would continue to rival cars such as the Nissan X-Trail and Vauxhall Grandland, it would instead target the heart of the market alongside the Nissan Qashqai and Vauxhall Frontera.

The result would be a simpler, more clearly defined SUV range, with each model occupying its own distinct role. Taken together, the e-Sky and new S-Cross show how Suzuki could strengthen its line-up without abandoning the qualities that have defined the brand for decades.

Compact dimensions, sensible engineering and dependable value would remain at the heart of every model, creating a range that feels more complete, more logical and better prepared for the years ahead.
