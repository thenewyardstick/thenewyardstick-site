The New Shape of McLaren — P47 Concept

TNY 7 Jul 2026

The McLaren P47 represents the boldest departure in the brand’s history: its first SUV, engineered as a new pillar for the marque under the stewardship of Bahrain’s Mumtalakat and Abu Dhabi’s CYVN Holdings. As McLaren broadens its product range, the challenge is to preserve the performance‑first DNA that has defined every model to date.

Early technical rumours point toward a twin‑turbo V8 hybrid powertrain derived from McLaren’s next‑generation MHP‑8 architecture - a system designed to deliver instant electric torque, high‑rev intensity, and the throttle precision expected of a true McLaren. The P47 is imagined as a taller, more versatile expression of the brand’s supercar philosophy, with proportions and aero sculpting that remain unmistakably McLaren.

Its mission is clear: to bring McLaren dynamics into a new segment, one now shaped by Ferrari’s Purosangue, without losing the identity that makes a McLaren feel alive. Our Renderings explore how the model could end up looking.

The P47 may be a major departure for McLaren, but it isn’t a compromise. It’s a strategic expansion: top‑tier performance paired with genuine everyday usability, giving the brand the commercial stability it needs to keep developing the supercars and hypercars that define its identity.

A model like this broadens McLaren’s appeal without diluting its engineering ethos, creating space for halo projects such as the recent W1 while strengthening long‑term profitability under its new ownership structure.

The future for McLaren looks increasingly promising - a brand evolving with the market, yet still driven by the same pursuit of performance that shaped its greatest cars.
