Cupra Guide

TNY 17 Jul 2026

Launched in 2018 as Seat's performance-focused sister brand, Cupra has established itself as a standalone manufacturer with a sportier, more premium image. Today, the range blends Volkswagen Group engineering with bold Spanish design, while newer electric models such as the Tavascan are built in China using Volkswagen's electric technology. The result is a distinctive line-up that combines proven German engineering, modern Chinese EV manufacturing and unmistakable Spanish styling, offering buyers something a little different from more conventional Volkswagen Group models.

Current Models

Cupra Leon (20-)

RRP From : £32,785
Pre Reg From : £27,500
Used Price From : £12,000

Powertrains : Petrol, PHEV
Dimensions : 4398mm × 1799mm × 1444mm
Boot : 380L,  270 (PHEV), 620L (Estate)

Key Rivals : Kia K4, Mazda 3, Peugeot 308, Seat Leon, Toyota Corolla, Volkswagen Golf

The Leon combines sharp styling with enjoyable handling and a practical interior. Available as both a hatchback and a more spacious estate, it offers a wide choice of petrol and plug-in hybrid powertrains. If performance styling isn't a priority, the closely related Seat Leon offers much of the same engineering for less money.

Cupra Born (21-)

RRP From : £32,995
Pre Reg From : £25,500
Used Price From : £11,000

Powertrains : EV
Dimensions : 4324mm × 1809mm × 1540mm
Boot : 385L

Key Rivals : Kia EV4, Leapmotor B05, MG4, Nissan Leaf, Volkswagen ID.3, 

Based on the Volkswagen ID.3, the Cupra Born combines strong performance with everyday usability and a more modern EV-first design than the comparable Leon. Rear-wheel drive and the dedicated EV platform give it a different driving feel to a conventional family hatchback, while steep initial depreciation means used examples can now be picked up for surprisingly little money.

Cupra Formentor (20-)

RRP From : £35,530
Pre Reg From : £26,500
Used Price From : £11,500

Powertrains : Petrol, PHEV
Dimensions : 4451mm × 1839mm × 1520mm
Boot : 450L (345L PHEV)

Key Rivals : Alfa Romeo Tonale, Mazda CX-30, Mercedes GLA, Renault Austral

The Leon-based Formentor remains Cupra's signature model, combining sporty hatchback driving dynamics with a higher driving position and distinctive crossover styling. Petrol and plug-in hybrid powertrains offer strong performance, making it an appealing choice for buyers who want something different from a conventional family hatchback

Cupra Terramar (24-)

RRP From : £38,015
Pre Reg From : £28,500
Used Price From : £25,000

Powertrains : Petrol, PHEV
Dimensions : 4519mm × 1869mm × 1586mm
Boot : 540L (490L PHEV)

Key Rivals : Hyundai Tucson, Kia Sportage, MG HS, Peugeot 3008, Volkswagen Tiguan

Cupra's largest petrol-powered SUV offers generous family space without losing the brand's sporty character. A high-tech interior, strong equipment levels and the availability of plug-in hybrid power make it a compelling choice for family buyers.

Cupra Tavascan (24-)

RRP From : £39,995
Pre Reg From : £30,500
Used Price From : £26,000

Powertrains : EV
Dimensions : 4644mm × 1861mm × 1597mm
Boot : 540L

Key Rivals : Tesla Model Y, Ford Capri, Peugeot E-3008, Volkswagen ID.5

If you're looking for a spacious family EV but don't want another Tesla Model Y, the Tavascan is worth considering. It combines proven Volkswagen Group electric technology with distinctive styling, generous equipment and plenty of passenger space.
