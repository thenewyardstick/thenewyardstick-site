DS Guide

TNY 17 Jul 2026

DS Automobiles brings a distinctly French approach to the premium car market, blending bold styling, luxurious interiors and a strong focus on comfort. The range places design and craftsmanship ahead of outright sportiness, with details inspired by French fashion, architecture and luxury goods helping every model stand apart. From compact crossovers to flagship electric SUVs, DS offers buyers something genuinely different, offering buyers a genuine alternative to more conventional premium cars.

Current Models

DS 3 (19-)

RRP From : £33,385
Pre Reg From : c. £29,000
Used Price From : c. £7,000

Powertrains : Mild Hybrid, EV
Dimensions : 4118mm x 1791mm x 1534mm
Boot : 350L

Key Rivals : Alfa Romeo Junior, Audi Q2, Lexus LBX, Mini Aceman, Volvo EX30

The DS 3 is a stylish small crossover that has never quite found the right balance between price, space and practicality. Unusually, it now costs more to buy new than the larger DS No4, making it difficult to justify on value alone. However, distinctive French design, premium materials and quirky styling details give it genuine character, while excellent used values make it one of the market's hidden gems as the current model approaches replacement.

DS No4 (21-)

RRP From : £32,200
Pre Reg From : c. £29,500
Used Price From : c. £11,000

Powertrains : Mild Hybrid, Plug-in Hybrid, EV
Dimensions : 4400mm x 1866mm x 1470mm
Boot : 430L (390L Plug-in Hybrid)

Key Rivals : BMW 1 Series, Kia EV4, Mercedes-Benz A-Class, Volkswagen ID.3

The No4 combines distinctive French styling with a refined, comfortable driving experience and one of the most distinctive interiors in its class. Available with mild hybrid, plug-in hybrid and fully electric power, it offers a refreshing alternative to more familiar premium hatchbacks, particularly for buyers looking for comfort, individuality and generous equipment over outright sporting appeal.

DS No7 (26-)

RRP From : £38,290
Pre Reg From : £38,290
Used Price From : £38,290

Powertrains : Mild Hybrid, EV
Dimensions : 4660mm x 1900mm x 1630mm
Boot : 560L

Key Rivals : Audi Q6 E-Tron, BMW iX3, Genesis GV70, Lexus NX, Volvo EX60

Replacing the long-serving DS 7, the new No7 introduces sharper styling, a more luxurious interior and a new generation of electrified powertrains. Entry-level mild-hybrid models keep the range accessible, while the flagship electric version delivers more than 450 miles of official range for around £50,000, setting a new benchmark for long-range family SUVs at this price point.

DS No8 (25-)

RRP From : £50,790
Pre Reg From : c. £50,000
Used Price From : c. £50,000

Powertrains : EV
Dimensions : 4820mm x 1900mm x 1580mm
Boot : 620L

Key Rivals : Audi A6 e-tron Sportback, BMW i5, Lexus RZ, Mercedes EQE, Polestar 4 

The No8 is DS's flagship electric model, blending the practicality of an SUV with the sleek styling of a fastback. Long-range batteries, generous interior space and a luxurious cabin give it genuine flagship appeal, while its distinctive design helps it stand apart from more conventional premium electric cars.
