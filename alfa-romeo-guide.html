Alfa Romeo Guide

TNY 15 Jul 2026

Founded in Milan in 1910, Alfa Romeo has built its reputation on distinctive Italian styling, engaging driving dynamics and characterful performance cars. Today, its range spans compact SUVs to executive saloons, with mild-hybrid and electric powertrains helping the historic marque embrace a new era of electrification.
Last updated: July 2026

Current Models

Junior (24-)

RRP From : £29,015
Pre Reg From : c. £ 22,000
Used Price From : c. £21,000
Powertrains : EV - MHEV
Dimensions :  4173mm x 1781mm x 1532mm
Boot : 415L (MHEV) • 400L (EV)
Key rivals : Ford Puma, Kia EV3, Peugeot 2008, Renault 4, Volvo EX30

With sharp handling, impressive practicality, efficient mild-hybrid and electric options, plus strong resale values, the Junior is easy to recommend. The 280hp Veloce adds genuine hot hatch excitement, making Alfa Romeo's smallest SUV one of the most characterful cars in its class.

Tonale (22-)

RRP From : £37,665
Pre Reg From : c. £ 26,500
Used Price From : c. £16,000
Powertrains : HEV - PHEV
Dimensions :  4522mm x 1841mm x 1601mm
Boot : 500L  (HEV) • 385L (PHEV)
Key rivals : Audi Q3, BMW X1, Lexus UX, Renault Austral, Volvo XC40

The facelifted Tonale builds on Alfa Romeo's stylish premium SUV with sharper exterior styling, improved interior quality and welcome new options, including red leather upholstery and elegant three-spoke alloy wheels. Efficient hybrid powertrains, composed handling and distinctive Italian character make it a refreshing alternative in the crowded premium SUV market.

Giulia (17-)

RRP From : £43,800
Pre Reg From : c. £37,995
Used Price From : c. £7,500
Powertrains : Petrol
Dimensions :  4643mm x 1860mm x 1436mm
Boot : 480L 
Key rivals : BMW 3-Series, Mercedes-Benz C-Class,

Despite its age, the Giulia remains one of the most rewarding executive saloons on sale. Timeless styling, rear-wheel drive and performance-focused petrol engines are increasingly rare today, while the departure of the Audi A4 and Jaguar XE has left the Giulia as one of the last true enthusiast-focused compact executive saloons.

Stelvio (17-)

RRP From : £52,120
Pre Reg From : c. £42,000
Used Price From : c. £10,000
Powertrains : Petrol - Diesel
Dimensions :  4687mm x 1903mm x 1671mm
Boot : 525L 
Key rivals : Audi Q5, BMW X3, Lexus NX, Mercedes GLC, Volvo XC60

The Stelvio continues to offer something increasingly rare among premium SUVs: genuine driver engagement. The range spans an efficient 2.2-litre diesel with impressive towing ability to the Quadrifoglio, whose Ferrari-derived V6 delivers sub-four-second 0-62mph performance. While rivals become increasingly digital, the Stelvio retains a refreshingly mechanical, driver-focused character.
